Here is my why

I am a girl from a farm home in Thailand. The farm made me deeply appreciate nature and love independence. I came to the US as a student in 2018, studying University of Washingtonâ€™s Global Business Program. Seattle's culture broadened my vision in powerful but contradictory ways.

On one hand, I saw companies get richer, and people become wealthier. On the other, I saw nature being destroyed for mindless consumption, driven by marketing by those same companies. I was devastated. That harsh reality brought me more clarity; I wanted to join a company that aims to lessen our environmental impact. So, I joined Scoop Marketplace - Seattle's zero-waste grocery store as a web editor and salesperson.

Here is my way

Working at Scoop helped me discover my joy in creating websites and want to develop a digital tool that simplifies zero-waste bulk shopping process; I decided to enroll in Programming Associate Degree at Seattle Central College. I hope to gain solid fundamental skills in programming, web design, building application. Also, I want to work with companies like, Ecosia, the search engine that plants trees or Package Free Shop, the shop that sells alternative products with minimal impact. I love what they do.

Although capitalism gives us power and freedom to consume, it also results in massive waste problems and global warming that has now gone too far...to the point of no return. I will feel like a responsible human being if I can contribute these powerful technical skills to the field that saves the world rather than destroying it.
